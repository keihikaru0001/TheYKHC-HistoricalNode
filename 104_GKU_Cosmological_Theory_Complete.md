## Abstract

The Genki-Koku Universe (GKU) theory is a formal cosmological framework that unifies the structure of spacetime, matter, energy, light, and economic value under a single ontological basis. The framework is built on two primitive concepts: **刻 (Koku)** — discrete temporal quanta as the irreducible unit of causality — and **元気 (Genki)** — the origin-energy field that constitutes all observable phenomena. The universe is modeled as a toroidal vessel (器, utsuwa) composed of four structural modes: 元 (G, Genki/origin), 空 (K, Ku/void), 気 (Q, Ki/flow), and 間 (M, Ma/interval). From these four primitives, GKU derives: (1) a discrete spacetime metric, (2) a matter existence criterion, (3) a wave-particle unification model for light, (4) a yin-yang asymmetric field compression mechanism, and (5) a temporal-gap credit protocol (Ma Protocol) applicable to economic systems. The theory is presented as a self-consistent formal structure with defined axioms, derived theorems, and testable predictions. All claims are clearly distinguished as either established physics (reproduced within GKU notation) or novel hypotheses requiring experimental verification.

**Keywords:** GKU, Genki-Koku Universe, discrete time, temporal quanta, koku, origin energy, toroidal cosmology, wave-particle unification, TheYKHC, unified field theory

## 1. Introduction: The Problem of Foundations

Modern physics rests on two pillars that remain mutually incompatible at their foundations: General Relativity (GR) and Quantum Mechanics (QM).

- **GR** treats spacetime as a continuous smooth manifold. Time is a coordinate, not a physical entity.
- **QM** treats physical quantities as discrete and probabilistic. Time appears as an external parameter, not an operator.

Every attempt at unification — string theory, loop quantum gravity, causal dynamical triangulation — must address the same fundamental question: **What is time?**

The GKU theory offers a specific answer:

> **Time is not a coordinate and not a parameter. Time is a structured field of discrete causal steps — 刻 (koku) — each step being the minimum interval of causal propagation in the local region of spacetime.**

From this answer, a complete cosmological framework is derived.

Time advances in irreducible discrete steps. There exists a minimum temporal quantum 刻 (koku) below which no causal distinction exists.

> ∃ τ_min > 0 such that no physical event can be causally distinguished within interval Δt < τ_min

This is analogous to, but distinct from, the Planck time. Planck time is a dimensional quantity arising from ℏ, G, c. Koku is a causal primitive — the minimum step of the causal order relation.

**Axiom 2 (Toroidal Closure):**  
The universe at every scale of description forms a closed toroidal structure (器, utsuwa) cycling through the four primitive modes G → K → Q → M → G.

> The universe is not an open system. Every structure — from a particle to a galaxy to the cosmos — is a closed cycle of the four primitives.

**Axiom 3 (Gradient Generation):**  
Where the density of koku steps τ(x) is non-uniform, a force field emerges directed from higher τ-density toward lower τ-density.

> **a** = −k∇τ(x)

This axiom encompasses gravity (as a special case where τ-gradient is induced by mass-energy concentration) and opens the possibility of non-gravitational τ-gradient sources (the basis of Phase Propulsion theory, Ref. [2]).

## 3. The Koku Field: Formal Definition

### 3.1 Local Proper Time as Koku Density

The density of koku steps at position x in a gravitational potential Φ(x) and with local velocity v(x) is:

**τ(x) = τ₀ × √(1 − 2Φ(x)/c² − v(x)²/c²)**

Where:
- τ₀ = reference koku density at coordinate infinity (flat spacetime, v=0)
- Φ(x) = gravitational potential at x (Φ = −GM/r for Schwarzschild exterior)
- v(x) = velocity of the local frame
- c = speed of light

This expression is the Schwarzschild proper time formula, here reinterpreted as the **local koku density** — the rate at which causal steps accumulate at position x.

**Physical consequences (all verified by experiment):**

| Phenomenon | GKU Interpretation | Experimental Verification |
|------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| GPS clock correction | τ higher at altitude (lower Φ), lower at speed | GPS system (1980–present) |
| Gravitational redshift | Photons climbing τ-gradient lose energy | Pound-Rebka (1959) |
| Time dilation in particle physics | High-v particles have lower τ | Muon lifetime (1941–present) |
| Perihelion precession | Orbit in non-uniform τ field deviates from Kepler | Mercury (GR prediction) |

GKU reproduces all established GR predictions exactly, because the koku density field is mathematically identical to the proper time field of GR. The distinction is ontological: GKU treats τ(x) as a physical field (capable of being locally modified), not merely a coordinate artifact.

### 3.2 The Koku Energy Quantum

Each discrete koku step carries a characteristic energy:

**Ef = β × Kf²**

Where:
- Ef = energy of a single koku quantum at frequency Kf
- Kf = koku frequency (= 1/τ_min in the local frame)
- β = GKU coupling constant (analogous to ℏ in quantum mechanics; value to be determined experimentally)

The structure of this equation parallels Einstein's photon energy E = hν. In GKU, the koku quantum is the temporal analogue of the photon — a discrete packet of causal propagation.

### 3.3 The Koku Interval (間, Ma)

Between two successive koku steps, there exists an interval M (間, Ma) — a state of pure potential in which no causal distinction exists. This interval is not zero; it is the "gap" between events that constitutes the fabric of temporal experience.

**Δφ = τ₀ − τ(x)**

The phase delay Δφ between the reference koku rate and the local koku rate is the GKU definition of gravitational and kinematic time dilation. It is also the theoretical basis for the Ma Credit Protocol (Section 6).

## 4. The Genki Field: Formal Definition

### 4.1 Origin-Energy

The 元 (Gen/Genki) field is the pre-differentiated energy density from which all observable phenomena emerge. It is not identical to vacuum energy (which is a QFT construct with specific renormalization properties) but is the GKU analogue of a source field.

**The Genki Index:**

**Ξ = ρ × C**

Where:
- Ξ (Xi) = Genki Index (dimensionless in normalized units)
- ρ = local energy density of the 元-field
- C = coherence factor (0 ≤ C ≤ 1; measures the degree of phase coherence of the local field)

**Physical interpretation:**

High Ξ: high-energy, highly coherent field region — corresponds to organized matter, stable structures, living systems

Low Ξ: low-energy or incoherent field region — corresponds to vacuum, thermal noise, dissipated structures

The Genki Index is GKU's measure of "structural integrity" — the degree to which a local region of the universe is organized vs. dissipated.

### 4.2 Matter Existence Criterion

In GKU, matter is not a primitive — it is a derived phenomenon. A stable material object exists when and only when:

**Ξ(x) > Ξ_threshold AND τ(x) forms a closed loop**

The second condition (τ-loop closure) means that the koku field at the location of the object must form a self-consistent closed cycle — matching Axiom 2 (toroidal closure). This is the GKU formulation of particle stability.

**Correspondence with standard physics:**
- Ξ > Ξ_threshold corresponds to the condition that rest mass energy exceeds the local vacuum fluctuation energy (particle vs. virtual particle distinction)
- τ-loop closure corresponds to the quantum number conservation laws that stabilize elementary particles

### 4.3 Yin-Yang Asymmetric Compression

The 元-field is inherently asymmetric. In toroidal geometry, the inner and outer surfaces of the torus have different curvatures and therefore different koku densities. This asymmetry drives the cycle G → K → Q → M → G:

**陰陽圧縮 (Yin-Yang Compression):**

> The inner surface (陰, Yin) of the toroidal vessel has higher τ-density (slower koku, more compressed time). The outer surface (陽, Yang) has lower τ-density (faster koku, more expanded time). The differential τ between inner and outer surfaces drives a continuous circulation of the 気 (Q) field — the physical manifestation of energy flow.

This mechanism is GKU's explanation for why the universe is not in thermal equilibrium — the toroidal asymmetry continuously generates a τ-gradient that drives energy flow, preventing maximum entropy.

## 5. Light: Wave-Particle Unification in GKU

### 5.1 The Problem

Standard physics describes light as:
- A wave (Maxwell's equations, interference, diffraction)
- A particle (photoelectric effect, Compton scattering, photon counting)

The wave-particle duality is accepted as a fundamental feature of quantum mechanics but is not derived from a more primitive principle — it is postulated.

### 5.2 GKU Derivation

In GKU, light is a specific mode of the 気 (Q) field propagating through the 空 (K) void-structure at the boundary condition imposed by the local τ(x) field.

**Wave behavior emerges when:**
The Q-field propagates through a region of uniform τ(x) — flat spacetime, no τ-gradient. The field spreads continuously, producing interference patterns.

**Particle behavior emerges when:**
The Q-field encounters a τ(x) boundary — a sharp gradient in koku density (e.g., at a detector, at a massive object, at a measurement event). The toroidal closure condition (Axiom 2) forces the field to localize into a discrete koku-energy packet Ef = β × Kf².

**Formal statement:**
> Light is a Q-field propagation mode. It manifests as a wave in uniform τ regions and as a particle (koku-energy quantum) at τ-boundary events.

**Whispering Gallery Mode (WGM) correspondence:**
In optical physics, WGM resonators demonstrate photon circulation in closed toroidal paths with near-unity efficiency. GKU identifies WGM as the physical laboratory instantiation of toroidal Q-field closure — the most direct experimental realization of Axiom 2.

**Physical Ma:** The interval between two causal events in which no causal distinction exists — pure potential.

**V(t) = V₀ × exp(∫₀ᵗ r(τ) dτ)**

In the Hikari Currency (光貨) implementation, r(τ) is determined by the ethical/moral quality of the action that generated the Mint event — linking the physics of koku density to the economics of moral value.
